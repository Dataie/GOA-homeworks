Khinkali

Khinkali is a traditional Georgian dumpling filled with minced meat, herbs, and spices. Typically made with beef, pork, or lamb, it’s known for its distinctive twisted top and juicy filling. Khinkali is usually boiled and eaten by hand, with the top being discarded after enjoying the rest of the dumpling. It's a popular dish in Georgia, especially in the mountainous regions.

Khachapuri

Khachapuri is a traditional Georgian dish made of a cheesy bread. The bread is typically stuffed with a mixture of cheese, eggs, and sometimes butter. The most popular version, Adjarian khachapuri, features a boat-shaped crust filled with cheese and topped with a raw egg and butter. It's often served hot and is a beloved comfort food in Georgia. Khachapuri is enjoyed throughout the country and is a symbol of Georgian hospitality.

kharcho

Kharcho is a traditional Georgian soup made with beef, rice, and a rich tomato-based broth. It is typically flavored with a blend of aromatic spices, including garlic, coriander, and vinegar, giving it a unique tangy and spicy taste. Often, walnuts and sour plum paste (tkemali) are added to enhance the depth of flavor. Kharcho is known for its hearty and comforting qualities, making it a popular dish, especially during cold weather. It's a classic part of Georgian cuisine, often served with fresh herbs and a side of bread.
